## QUICK PRICE COMPARISON TOOL

### For Pricing Decisions: Quick Reference

**When deciding on pricing for new feature/tier:**

1. Find your product category above
2. Identify the tier you're pricing
3. Note your position vs incumbent (budget/mid/premium)
4. Apply 15-20% discount for annual vs monthly
5. Add 15-25% premium if bundled with other products

**Example Calculation:**

```
New: "LinkedIn + Transcription Bundle"

Individual pricing:
- LinkedIn Agent: $79/month
- Transcription: $16/user/month (avg 5 users = $80)
- Total: $159/month

Bundle pricing (18% off):
- $159 × 0.82 = $130/month
- Marketing message: "Save $29/month bundled"

Annual committed pricing (additional 20%):
- $130 × 0.80 = $104/month ($1,248/year)
- Customer perception: "Save $660/year" or "5+ months free"
```

## PRICE ELASTICITY FORMULAS

### How to Adjust Pricing Without Losing Market Share

**Safe price increase:** +10-15% annually
- Mid-market accepts with product improvements + feature additions
- Lock in with multi-year contracts

**Aggressive price increase:** +20-25% annually
- Requires either: major feature release OR customer success stories
- Risk: 15-25% churn unless offset by lock-in

**Discount strategy:** -15% for multi-year commitment
- Converts $100 ARR to $85, but locks in 3+ years = $255 vs $300 (net better)
- Reduces churn-related revenue loss
